About team

We chose these five exoplanets — Proxima b, Ross 128 b, HD 85512 b, Gliese 667Cc, and Kepler-186f — for good reason. Each of these planets plays an important role in studying and understanding space and has the potential to address several real-world issues. The project we are undertaking has several key objectives and brings significant benefits both to the scientific community and to the popularization of space research.

The main goal of our project is to investigate potentially habitable exoplanets. There is a real problem in finding planets beyond Earth that could support life. The five planets we selected are located in the habitable zone of their stars, where the existence of liquid water is possible, which is a crucial factor for the development of life. We are creating a platform that systematizes and analyzes data about these planets to help scientists better understand which of them could become targets for future missions. In the context of climate change and resource depletion on Earth, the search for potentially habitable worlds becomes particularly relevant.

Our project also helps to advance research in astrophysics. Each of these planets presents a unique interest to science. For example, Proxima b is the closest exoplanet to us, orbiting in the habitable zone of its star, while Kepler-186f is the first Earth-sized planet discovered in the habitable zone of another star. These planets allow scientists to explore the nature of planets beyond our solar system and deepen our understanding of the universe.

One of the important aspects of our project is its educational component. We aim to raise awareness among the general public about space research and make complex scientific data more accessible to understanding. Through our website and project, we demonstrate why it is important to study exoplanets, how this can impact the future of humanity, and how such research can inspire future generations of scientists and researchers.

Exoplanets Information

• Pxoxima Centauri b

Mass: 1.17 Earth masses

Distance from Earth: 4.24 light years

Proxima Centauri b is the closest exoplanet to Earth. It orbits in the habitable zone of the star Proxima Centauri, which may allow it to support liquid water. Scientists are still studying its atmosphere and surface conditions.

• Ross 128 b

Mass: 1.35 Earth masses

Distance from Earth: 11 light years

Ross 128 b is located 11 light years away from Earth and is one of the closest potenrially habitable planets. It orbits a red dwarf star and could have a surface temperature that allows for liquid water.

• HD 85512 b

Mass: 3.6 Earth masses

Distance from Earth: 36 light years

HD 85512 b is an exoplanet located around 36 light years away from Earth. It orbits its star in the habitable zone, making it another cadidate for potential life.

• Gliese 677Cc

mASS: 4.5 Earth masses

Distance from Earth: 23.62 light years

Gliese 677Cc is a super-Earth that orbits in the habitable zone of the red dwarf star Gliese 677Cc. It is a strong candidate for habitability due to its location and size.

• Kepler-186f

Mass: 1.0 Earth masses

Distance from Earth: 500 light years

Kepler-186f is the first Earth-sized planet discoreved in the habitable zone of its star. It is located about 500 light years away in the Kepler-186 system and could potentially have conditions similar to Earth.
